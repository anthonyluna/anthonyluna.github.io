How we met

Simone's Perspective:

Anthony and I met on the day before graduate studies began. It was a fall evening in the school courtyard and our entire cohort was there to meet our fellow classmates. The moment I saw Anthony I was so curious to meet him! I played it cool and we finally bumped into each other as the sun was setting. We hit it off right away. We talked for a while about travels and family…

After that, I'd see him in classes or around campus, and each time my heart would speed up a bit. But it wasn't too startling because we were both so busy that we hardly saw each other. Occasionally, we would see each other in the break room and Anthony would always have fruit fresh off a tree to share. He'd silently open up a handful of persimmons and trail mix when I saw him and I'd stand and munch with him until one of us had to go somewhere.

It went like that for at least a few months until we finally made a “date” to study together in the library. It didn't matter that winter break had just begun and no assignment was really that urgent or that a fire was blazing its way into Santa Barbara. We sat and studied together for 4 hours. It felt like a study standoff. Finally, we called it quits and said goodbye. I regretted it was over, although I was not so sad that I no longer needed to focus my attention on economics.

After walking home that same evening, I got a text from Anthony inviting me to dinner and we went out on our first date – which was amazingly relaxed, especially considering that Anthony's home had become part of the evacuation zone. We said our goodnights and planned to meet after winter break, and Anthony kindly wrote down his folks' address, in case I too was evacuated later that evening.

And it went like that. Lots of things could have torn us apart, but instead they brought us together. The rest is a unique tapestry of finishing school, navigating a pandemic, getting to know each other as friends, colleagues, housemates, and now looking forward into a long future together.I would say it was “love at first sight” because he is so handsome, but I think I fall in love with Anthony over and over again, because it seems like we are always changing and meeting each other anew.

Anthony's Perspective:

Simone and I first met at the tail end of a mixer at the tail end of our Master's program orientation. I asked if she was a PhD student, because I hadn't seen her around all week to which she laughed and said she was a first year like me. She then revealed that she had just returned from a trip in France to visit her good friend Grace Williams, which I thought that was a great reason to miss orientation. I then felt obligated to speak at Simone in French as you do after a few drinks, especially when trying to swoon and impress a beautiful woman. I didn't want to stop our lively conversation, and so asked if she wanted to continue the mixer with other fellow grad students at a local bar - she declined and left me yearning for another encounter.

During our first few weeks of classes, I saw Simone and a few classmates in the common room kitchen clamouring to finish a coding assignment. I already had experience with the computer programming language we were using, The R Language, so I joined the group to lend a hand. This is when Simone and I first exchanged contact info, and I was known to her as "The R Extraordinaire". I was elated at the prospect of studying with her again - she made everything more fun, even coursework.

Another week past, and we both found ourselves at a networking event with around 20 of our classmates in downtown Santa Barbara. Afterwards, a group were going to go to a house party down the street, and Simone and I found each other. We started walking and talking and found ourselves separated from the group. I thought she knew where we were going, but after our second strange turn I realized we were completely lost - but neither of us minded. We talked about family played word games with each other while walking through the neighborhoods near State street. Simone picked flowers as we walked, and placed them carefully in my hair. No one had ever done that to me before and I loved it.

After this night, I knew we needed to go on a date - a proper date, not one under the guise of a networking event or a homework assignment, but I didn't know when the right time was to ask. I brought her snacks every day so I had a reason to chat, but never got too into the one on one since we were busy with classes. Then one day we worked on an assignment for at least 7 hours. To be honest, I had been done with the assignment for a while, but I continued to format the document needlessly as we chatted and clacked away on our keyboards. Finally, she said she was done, and I said me too, then as we walked down the hall she said "I have to use the restroom" and I said awkwardly "Oh, okay, have a good night..." thinking maybe she was trying to give me a hint to buzz off. A bit disappointed, I walked to the bus stop, got on my bus, sat down and realized I was an absolute fool. I texted my sister Shamsi, and she confirmed that yes, I was an idiot. I texted Simone very casually if she wanted to get dinner (thanks to Shamsi's guidance) and thankfully she said yes. It was a great night, one I'll never forget. I began to fall in love with Simone the first time I met her, and have been falling more and more for her every day since.

The Venue

Where are we headed to celebrate:

Camp Arnaz is located in the lower Ojai Valley and inland from the City of Ventura. Camp Arnaz is named after Actor Desi Arnaz who previously owned the property, which was purchased by the Girl Scouts of CA Central Coast in 1975. Camp Arnaz has recently been designated as a Monarch Breeding and Research Institute with pollinator gardens. It is also home to a variety of wildlife, including red-eared turtles, herons, fish, squirrels, owls, blue jays, swallows, woodpeckers, red tailed hawks and more!
